<div class="project-text reveal">

The building's structural system employs a hybrid approach combining post-tensioned concrete flat plates for the residential floors with structural steel framing at the ground-level retail and amenity spaces. This combination allowed for the column-free retail spans the architect envisioned while maintaining the efficient floor-to-floor heights required for 380 residential units above.

The foundation system presented its own challenges. Situated adjacent to the East River, the site required extensive dewatering and a secant pile wall system to manage hydrostatic pressures during construction. CityScape's support of excavation design enabled the three levels of below-grade parking without disrupting the neighboring properties.

</div>

<div class="project-text reveal">

Wind engineering was a critical consideration at this height and exposure. Working with the project's wind consultant, we optimized the lateral system using a concrete core with outrigger trusses at the mechanical floors, reducing the building's drift to well within code limits while minimizing material usage.

The result is a structure that performs efficiently, builds cleanly, and gives the architect the flexibility they needed to create a landmark residential tower on the Queens waterfront.

</div>
