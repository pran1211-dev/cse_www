Brownstone renovations are among the most rewarding — and most structurally complex — projects in New York City. These 19th-century row houses have survived over a century of use, but their original construction methods present challenges that modern renovation teams must navigate carefully.

## Common Structural Issues

Most brownstones were built with unreinforced masonry bearing walls, timber floor framing, and shallow foundations. Over time, settlement, water damage, and previous unpermitted alterations can compromise the structural integrity of these buildings.

## Opening Up Floor Plans

One of the most common requests from brownstone owners is removing walls to create open living spaces. In a bearing wall structure, this requires careful engineering to transfer loads to new beams and columns without disrupting the load path to the foundation.

## Foundation Concerns

Brownstone foundations were typically built on rubble stone with lime mortar. Underpinning or reinforcing these foundations is often necessary for major renovations, particularly when adding a cellar level or increasing floor loads.
